**Abstract:** The rapid expansion of high-resolution cryo-EM structures of amyloid fibrils has transformed our understanding of fibril polymorphism, yet it has not been matched by comparable progress in the rational development of protein-selective or polymorph-specific amyloid ligands. One possible explanation is that ligand selectivity is governed not only by global fibril folds, but also by local surface pockets accessible to small molecules. Here, we present a systematic analysis of 400 cryo-EM structures of amyloid-{beta}, tau, and -synuclein fibrils. Using a unified pocket similarity index and minimum spanning tree representations, we construct global and protein-specific graph representations of the amyloid binding pocket space, and examine how surface cavities are distributed across proteins, polymorphs, and structural contexts.
